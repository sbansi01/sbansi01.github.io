Hello, I'm Sapna!


I'm a journalist who also worked in healthcare for nearly a decade.


Driven by my passions for reporting and storytelling, I switched careers and enrolled in the journalism program at the University of Maryland. I expect to graduate in May 2024.

I've used my experiences in healthcare to write about health policy as a state house reporter in Annapolis. As a lifelong sports fan, I also like to profile compelling people and write about important societal issues through the prism of sports. I especially love to cover women's sports.

I've written about a range of other subjects: transit, culture, criminal justice and elections, among others. My work has appeared in The Washington Post, The Baltimore Banner, The Baltimore Sun and The Philadelphia Inquirer.

I am also developing skills as a data journalist and learning R, Python, HTML and CSS. Most recently, I was the lead data reporter for a six-month investigation into youth tackle football that was published by The Associated Press and aired on PBS NewsHour.
